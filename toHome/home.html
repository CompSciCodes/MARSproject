MARS ↑ Project

The Future Is Now ... So Let Us Begin!


You are on a mission to MARS: While on your mission, you will have many tasks based on your career field. Each of you will have the opportunity to demonstrate your new found abilities obtained through research and teamwork. You will learn many skills along the way. Get ready, as it will be a busy time with many deadlines to meet!

I am excited that you are joining me on this endeavor. This mission will last approximately nine months, from September through May. Some of you are programmers, while others of you are computer IT experts. Each and every role will be an important one. Expectations will be high, as this will determine our success. Are you ready?

As a mission specialist,you will be responsible for a variety of duties to include:

  • Apply your expertise toward improving the overall quality of life on MARS.
  • Work in a team environment to insure success by all its members.
  • Commit to submitting all tasks within specified deadlines.
  • Record any problems encountered that hinder progress of a project.
  • Communicate effectively about the transition to new technologies.
  • Include technical drawings, blueprints, manuals, or flowcharts of your solutions.
  • Provide continued training and technical support to all users.
  • Maintain professionalism and ethics while in the work environment.

These expectations shall be maintained at all times until the end of this mission. I hope you are ready for the many challenges that await you. Together, we can create a sustainable and innovative community on MARS. I am looking forward to spending the year with you! Ms. Jansen

ONE

Your 1st Task



When you first get to Mars, a lot of learning will need to take place in order to understand what is needed to survive. Research the red planet using these questions as your guide:

  • What are the day/night temperatures?
  • What makes up the atmosphere?
  • What elements make up the soil?
  • How much do you weigh on Mars?
  • How long are the days?
  • How many days are in a year?

Enter the results in your mission journal, then, add two more questions to your research!
 TWO

Your 2nd Task



In the beginning, you will have a very small dwelling in which to live. You are given some freedom to its design, and how you want it arranged. Include all the necessary spaces used on earth, considering:

  • eating arrangements
  • personal hygiene
  • sleeping and studying
  • entertainment and exercise
  • storage space

Your dome has a diameter of 15 feet. You will be limited to that space for all your belongings.
 THREE

Your 3rd Task



A ship with an abundant supply of food from earth has arrived. Eating balanced meals will be necessary for your overall health, along with emotional well-being and exercise. Create a healthy menu for the week with a variety of foods from each of the major food groups:

  • Fruit
  • Vegetable
  • Grain
  • Protein
  • Dairy

Plan for three meals a day. Use the Eatwell Guide to help you with this task.

FOUR

Your 4th Task



A hierarchy is established in the colony, and specialist roles are identified. Domes are also established. So everyone knows who to go to and where they live, several charts need to be provided with this information. Create a tree chart displaying everyone's responsibilities and who they report to, and a node map with connecting hallways referencing living habitats, work areas, a cafe, and a community restroom.

See further instructions for colony member names, roles, and node information. A great tool for this is Google Draw!
FIVE

Your 5th Task



You have been tasked with taking pictures of your site to send back to earth. The site is near Jezero Crater. Explore the following facts about this location:

  • diameter and depth of the crater
  • which rover landed in the crater
  • what earth was hoping to discover
  • why it was named Jezero
  • the minerals making up the rocks and soil

Your photos will help support your discoveries so the colony can begin the plan for cultivation. This image generator can help! Use a slide presentation to display the photos/descriptions.

SEVEN

Your 7th Task



You finally have some time to write back home! A few months on Mars can be daunting for anyone, but you have managed to successfully get over some difficult hurdles in your journey. Share the differences between living on Earth versus Mars. Some ideas for discussion can be:

  • what you see and hear
  • how you spend your time
  • living conditions
  • emotional well-being
  • what you have learned

Unfortunately, you are limited to 250 characters to speed up the transmission time to earth. Use this counter to help you keep track!

Your 9th Task



As colonization increases, life will be forced to expand out to other areas on Mars. When this occurs, a transit system will need to be planned. Determine the distances and the best routes between each of these regions:

  • Jezero Crater
  • Syrtis
  • Isidis
  • Planitia
  • Planum

Create a map of the area, and sketch roadways between each region, and note the distances.
